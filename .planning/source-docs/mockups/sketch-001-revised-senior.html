Overview ✎ Edit
Dr. Whitcomb is the Stephen & Suzanne Weiss Chair of the Department of Medicine and a Professor of Medicine at Weill Cornell Medicine, where she has served on the faculty since 1992. Trained as both a clinician and an epidemiologist, her research has focused on three intertwined questions: how to make hospital care safer, how to translate clinical-trial evidence into routine practice, and how to address persistent inequities in the quality of care delivered across patient populations. Her early work established several of the methodological foundations for modern hospital quality measurement, including risk-adjusted mortality models that have since been adopted by CMS and the Joint Commission. Over the past two decades she has led pragmatic clinical trials in antibiotic stewardship, transitions of care, and inpatient decision support, with continuous NIH and AHRQ funding since 1996. As Department Chair since 2019 she has overseen the strategic expansion of the department's hospital medicine, geriatrics, and digital health programs, and she chairs the WCM Faculty Affairs and Promotions Committee. Dr. Whitcomb has been elected to the American Society for Clinical Investigation, the Association of American Physicians, and the National Academy of Medicine. She has trained more than 60 physician-scientists, many of whom now lead departments and research programs across U.S. academic medical centers.
