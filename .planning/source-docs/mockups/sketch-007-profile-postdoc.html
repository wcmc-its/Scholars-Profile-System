Overview
Marcus Vargas joined the Ramirez Lab in 2024 as a postdoctoral associate after completing his PhD at UCSF, where he studied cellular senescence in muscle stem cells. His current work focuses on senescence biology in cardio-oncology — specifically, the molecular mechanisms by which anthracycline chemotherapy induces senescence in cardiomyocytes, and whether senolytic interventions can prevent or reverse cardiotoxicity. He is supported by an NIH F32 fellowship.
