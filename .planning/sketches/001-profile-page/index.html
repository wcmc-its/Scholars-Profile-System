Overview ✎ Edit

Dr. Ramirez is the Chief of the Division of Cardiology and the Sandra A. Daugherty Professor of Cardiology at Weill Cornell Medicine. Her research focuses on cardio-oncology — the cardiovascular complications of cancer therapies — and on understanding why heart disease presents differently in women than men. She leads the Women's Heart Health Program at NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital and directs an NIH-funded research program investigating cardioprotective strategies for patients receiving immune checkpoint inhibitors and anthracycline-based chemotherapy. Her clinical practice emphasizes long-term cardiovascular surveillance for cancer survivors, with particular attention to the unique presentations of cardiotoxicity in women.
