A2 · With the article + ReCiter confidence score (recommended)

Showing the recognizable title and the score (styled like ReCiter’s own chip) is the biggest conversion lever — the scholar sees their own paper, not an abstract count — and it markets ReCiter twice: the chip reinforces the brand and a high score advertises the engine’s precision.

A3 · Verbose vs. referred-to — the score threshold

Reuse ReCiter’s own score bands (authorshipLikelihoodScore, 0–100; XGBoost probability → calibrated → ×100). ReCiter only “suggests” at ≥ 30 for curators; for a self-serve scholar we hold a higher bar so we never nag about a borderline match.

≥ 70
Strong
Verbose — full citation card + green score chip + tooltip (the hero shown in A2).
40–69
Moderate
Referred-to — count + plain title list (“N possible publications”), no hero card, softer copy.
30–39
Weak
Don’t nudge the scholar — leave these to library curation in ReCiter.

Bands are ReCiter’s (PM colors green ≥70 / amber 40–69 / gray <40). The only SPS-specific call is the nudge floor: skip 30–39 so the self-serve surface stays high-precision. Raise to ≥50 if early feedback is noisy.

Score-chip tooltip (hover preview — describes the score only, no curation claim):

Authorship confidence — 100 / 100. ReCiter’s empirically-derived estimate of how likely this paper is yours, based on your name, affiliations, co-authors, topics and grants. Higher means more certain.

Curation context lives in the banner copy, not the score tooltip (plain text, not red): “Candidate articles like this are often accepted or rejected by WCM library curators on scholars’ behalf; the ones still pending may include papers of yours that aren’t on your profile yet.”
