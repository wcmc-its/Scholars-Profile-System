## Message body (~210 words)

**Subject:** Heads-up — real Scholars requests start landing in 3 weeks

Hi all,

We're entering the launch window for `scholars.weill.cornell.edu`. Over the next three weeks, outreach goes out to center admins, pilot department admins, then all faculty — which means real users will start filing slug requests, correction requests, and "why does my profile say X" questions. A few things to have ready:

**Your operating manual is KB Article 3** (`docs/kb/03-superusers.md`): request triage, the slug-request queue, unit-admin grants/revocations, and the known data quirks. Skim it before Wave 2.

**What's coming your way:**

- **Slug requests** — review and approve/decline in `/edit/slug-requests`. Collisions and reserved words are flagged; a decline needs a note (the requester sees it).
- **Correction requests** — most route automatically to the owning office via **Request a change**; see the routing in [`../feedback-handling-matrix.md`](../feedback-handling-matrix.md). The Scholars-team queue catches ReciterAI-computed fields, center membership, identity/duplicate issues, and "can't find an answer."
- **Hide / takedown asks** — scholars hide their own items; site-wide publication removal and admin profile-hide are superuser actions and require an audit reason.

**On-call:** at least one of us is reachable on Teams for the first 72 hours of each wave. Watch this channel.

Questions or anything unclear in Article 3 — reply here.

[Sender]
