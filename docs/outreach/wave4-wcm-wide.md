## Bulletin item

> **Headline:** Scholars Profile System now publicly available at scholars.weill.cornell.edu

The Scholars Profile System — a public-facing showcase of research, scholarship, and clinical work across Weill Cornell Medicine — is now live at **scholars.weill.cornell.edu**.

Scholars lets anyone search for WCM experts by topic, browse the work happening in a department or center, and view individual research portfolios assembled from authoritative WCM sources.

**If you're WCM faculty, a postdoctoral fellow, a clinical or research fellow, or affiliated faculty**, you already have a profile. Sign in at `scholars.weill.cornell.edu/edit` with your CWID to review what's there, edit your professional overview, hide items you don't want to surface, or request a custom URL. Full guide: {{KB_ARTICLE_1_URL}}.

**Public FAQ** for visitors and the broader community: {{PUBLIC_FAQ_URL}}.

Questions about your profile data, or about a profile you're trying to find? Contact the WCM Service Desk.

## Notes for ITS Communications

- **Treat as a service-launch announcement, not a campaign.** This goes out alongside other infrastructure updates; it does not need a marketing push.
- **External press** is a separate decision the Communications office owns. Scholars project does not author or initiate external announcements.
- **Headline matters more than body.** Most bulletin readers will scan the headline and the first sentence; build for that. Recommended headline above leads with the site URL because that's the actionable bit.
- **Drop the "If you're WCM faculty…" paragraph** if the bulletin format strongly favors one-paragraph items. The faculty already received Wave 2 outreach two weeks earlier and don't strictly need a second prompt here.

## What this message intentionally does not contain

- A long methodology explanation. The `/docs` page in the Scholars site itself handles that.
- Promotional language ("the most comprehensive…", "groundbreaking…"). Service-launch tone.
- A specific count of profiles, publications, or scholars. Numbers move; making one true on launch day means we have to keep it current. Refer to the site itself for current counts.
- The list of upstream data sources. KB Article 1 has that and the public FAQ summarizes.
- A "feedback welcome" line. The feedback badge (#538) is the durable feedback surface; the bulletin doesn't need to recruit.
