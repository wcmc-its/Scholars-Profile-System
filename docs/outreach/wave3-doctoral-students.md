## Email body (Option B — students hidden at launch)

> **Subject:** Scholars launches {{LAUNCH_DATE}} — what this means for doctoral students

Dear doctoral students,

On **{{LAUNCH_DATE}}**, the Scholars Profile System (`scholars.weill.cornell.edu`) becomes publicly accessible. Scholars is a public-facing showcase of WCM research and scholarship.

**At launch, doctoral students do not have public Scholars profiles.** This is a deliberate decision — the Scholars Profile System is scoped to faculty and research staff for its initial release. You do not need to take any action.

**Where you may still appear:** your name appears as a mentee on your advisor's profile (as plain text, not as a link to a profile of your own) and on co-author lists for publications you've worked on. This is contextual and not a directed-traffic surface.

**If your role changes** during your time at WCM — for example, you transition to a postdoctoral position — your Scholars profile will become available automatically on the next nightly refresh after the role change is recorded in the institutional directory. We'll surface that change to you at the time; no action is required from you now.

**If you have questions** about how you appear on Scholars or want to flag something specific, contact the Scholars project via the Service Desk. Full background: {{KB_ARTICLE_1_URL}}.

— {{SENDER_NAME}}

## Notes for whoever sends this

- **Do not over-explain why students are excluded.** The KB Article 1 link is enough for anyone who wants the rationale. The email itself should be informational, not defensive.
- **The "where you may still appear" paragraph is important.** Students who read this will reasonably ask "so where am I visible at all?" — answer that question in the message itself rather than letting them go hunting.
- **Watch for inbound from students who explicitly want a profile.** Document these as input for a future scope expansion. Do not promise individual exceptions at launch.
