## Message body (~230 words)

**Subject:** You're piloting department curation on the new WCM Scholars site

Hi [name],

Thanks for agreeing to pilot the department-curation tools on the new WCM Scholars site (`scholars.weill.cornell.edu`) before it goes public. You've been granted **Owner** access for **[department / division]**, and I'd like you to try it this week so we can fix anything awkward before the broad faculty rollout.

Sign in at `scholars.weill.cornell.edu/edit` and open your department page from there.

**What you can curate directly:**

- The department's **description** and **leadership**.
- **Highlighted** faculty/publications and the **order** they appear in.
- Informal **subunits / rosters** that aren't captured upstream.
- Grant **Curator** access (curate-only) to colleagues who help maintain the page — via "Manage access" in the edit view.

**What you can't change here, and where it does get changed:**

- The structural hierarchy (which divisions roll up to which department) — that stays with Scholars superusers.
- Faculty appointments, titles, and primary departments — managed in the **Web Directory** / Faculty Affairs, not here.

Full detail is in the SOP (KB Article 2), attached/linked.

**If something's wrong and you can't fix it,** use **Request a change** on the page, or reply to me directly during the pilot.

Could you try it and send me a note on anything confusing by [date]? I'll fold your feedback in before Wave 2.

[Sender]
