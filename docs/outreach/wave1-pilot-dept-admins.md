## Email body

> **Subject:** Scholars pilot — try the department curation tools before {{LAUNCH_DATE}}

Dear colleagues,

Thank you for agreeing to be the first group to try the Scholars department-curation tools. We're three weeks from the public launch of Scholars on **{{LAUNCH_DATE}}** and your hands-on feedback over the next week will shape how the rest of the WCM administrator community gets onboarded.

**What we're asking of you:**

1. **Read KB Article 2** ({{KB_ARTICLE_2_URL}}) — it explains the three-tier role model (Superuser / Owner / Curator) and walks through what you can and can't change about your unit page. You already have Owner access on your subtree.

2. **Try the curation workflow.** Sign in at `scholars.weill.cornell.edu`, navigate to your department, division, or center page, and try: promoting a publication to Highlights, featuring a few scholars, suppressing an item from display, and (if it applies) creating an informal subunit. Walkthrough: {{CURATION_WALKTHROUGH_URL}}.

3. **Tell us what's confusing or broken.** Use the short feedback form at {{FEEDBACK_FORM_URL}} — capture anything that surprised you, didn't work, or made you think "this isn't what an admin would expect." Be specific. Negative feedback is more useful than positive.

4. **Join a 30-minute retrospective call on {{RETRO_CALL_DATE}}.** We'll synthesize what came in through the feedback form and decide what to change before the broader scholar outreach goes out at T-2 weeks.

**What you can and can't do at the unit level** is summarized in Article 2; the short version is: you control curation and informal subunit creation; structural hierarchy and faculty appointments still flow from OFA and the Enterprise Directory.

If you hit a wall, contact me directly rather than the Service Desk during the pilot — the Service Desk hasn't been briefed yet (KB Article 4 is in their queue but they don't have rep-by-rep training until T-1 week).

Thanks for the time.

— Omar
